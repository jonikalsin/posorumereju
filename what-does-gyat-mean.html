What does GYAT mean? The TikTok slang term explained

If you’re a TikTok user, you might have come across the term GYAT in some videos or comments.

But what does it mean and where did it come from? In this article, we’ll break down everything you need to know about this viral slang term.

What does the slang GYAT mean on TikTok?

GYAT is an abbreviation for “goddamn”, usually said in a fast and aggressive way. It’s often used to express admiration or attraction for someone with a curvy body type, especially a woman with a large butt.

The term is also spelled as GYATT, GYATTT, GYAAT, or GYAATT, depending on how emphatic the speaker wants to be.

There is an alternative definition for ‘gyat,’ which suggests that the word means “get your act together.”

It refers to instructing someone to behave more appropriately or effectively. However, you probably won’t see this as often as the former meaning.

The origin of GYAT can be traced back to Twitter, where users like @azzman and Nicki Minaj used it as a variation of “gyat damn” in 2009 and 2012 respectively.

However, the term became more popular in 2021, thanks to the Twitch streamer YourRage, who would shout “GYATT” whenever he saw a hot girl on his screen.

His fans and chat would then spam the word accordingly.

From Twitch, GYAT made its way to TikTok, where it became a common meme and an inside joke among the guys.

Many TikTokers use GYAT in their captions, text overlays, or comments to show their appreciation for someone they find attractive.

Some also use the “GYATT” sound that features YourRage’s voice saying the word.

Conclusion

GYAT is a slang term that means “goddamn” and is used to compliment someone with a curvy physique.

It originated from Twitter and was popularized by Twitch streamer YourRage.

It has since become a viral meme on TikTok, where many users say it or write it when they see someone they like.
