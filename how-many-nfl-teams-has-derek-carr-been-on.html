How many NFL teams has Derek Carr been on?

Derek Dallas Carr, born on March 28, 1991, is a prominent American football quarterback currently playing for the New Orleans Saints of the National Football League (NFL).

Throughout his career, Carr has been associated with two NFL teams, namely the Las Vegas Raiders (formerly Oakland Raiders) and the New Orleans Saints. 

Early Life and College Career

Derek Carr's journey in football began during his college years at Fresno State, where he played for the Fresno State Bulldogs from 2009 to 2013.

During his time at Fresno State, Carr received numerous accolades, including being selected by the Oakland Raiders in the second round of the 2014 NFL draft. His exceptional performance during his college years set the stage for his entry into the professional football arena.

Career with the Las Vegas Raiders

Carr's NFL career commenced with the Oakland Raiders, now known as the Las Vegas Raiders, where he played from 2014 to 2022.

During his tenure with the Raiders, Carr made a significant impact, achieving notable milestones and setting franchise records.

Notably, he led the Raiders to the playoffs during the 2016 season, marking the franchise's first appearance since 2002.

His prolific performance on the field earned him four Pro Bowl selections and several prestigious awards, including the Sammy Baugh Trophy and the Mountain West Conference Offensive Player of the Year accolades.

Carr's impressive statistics with the Raiders include 5,506 passing attempts, 3,576 passing completions, a completion percentage of 64.9%, 242 touchdowns, 107 interceptions, and a total of 39,100 passing yards as of 2023.

He left the Raiders as the franchise leader in passing yards, passing touchdowns, and pass completions, solidifying his legacy with the team.

Transition to the New Orleans Saints

In 2023, Derek Carr embarked on a new chapter in his NFL career by joining the New Orleans Saints. His move to the Saints marked a pivotal moment in his professional journey, signifying a fresh start with a new team. Carr's transition to the Saints brought about heightened anticipation and speculation regarding his performance with his new team, as well as the impact he would have on the Saints' roster.
